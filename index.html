PAMOJA OUTREACH FOUNDATION

Give Hope To Children In Need.

Pamoja Outreach Foundation is a Kampala-based NGO that stands with vulnerable children, elderly persons, and families in underserved communities through practical, dignity-restoring support.

Many households in ghetto areas carry the weight of poverty, illness, and isolation. We come alongside them with consistent care, education support, and compassionate follow-up so that no one has to struggle alone.

We are here to listen carefully, act responsibly, and walk with families for the long term.

WHO WE ARE

About Pamoja Outreach Foundation

Pamoja Outreach Foundation is a registered NGO based in Kampala, Uganda, led by Mr. Akuma Jamal and committed to serving elderly persons, orphans, financially constrained individuals, the sick, and persons with disabilities in vulnerable communities.

Our Mission

To identify, support, and empower the most vulnerable children, elderly persons, and families in Kampala’s underserved communities through holistic care, education, medical support, and practical social assistance rooted in dignity and compassion.

We exist to stand with people who are often forgotten in the ghetto areas of Kampala—offering consistent, practical support that restores hope and protects dignity.

Our Mission

Our mission is to provide holistic support to vulnerable children, elderly persons, and families through orphan care, elderly care, medical assistance, and education sponsorship, working hand in hand with local communities.

Our Vision

Our vision is a community where every vulnerable person is seen, supported, and given a fair opportunity to live a healthy, dignified, and hope-filled life.

Our Core Values

  • Dignity: We treat every person with respect, regardless of their circumstances.
  • Integrity: We act honestly and responsibly in every decision and interaction.
  • Accountability: We handle resources and relationships with care, transparency, and stewardship.
  • Compassion: We respond to need with empathy, patience, and practical support.
  • Community: We work alongside local leaders, families, and volunteers to create lasting change.

Maternal Health Outreach

Medical Welfare Education for Women & Mothers

Community-led sessions provide practical guidance on prenatal care, hygiene, nutrition, and early warning signs, delivered in simple language and local context.

Follow-up referrals and peer groups increase clinic attendance, support safer pregnancies, and build confidence at home.

HEAR FROM OUR DIRECTOR

Hear From Our Director

A personal note from the leadership of Pamoja Outreach Foundation about responsibility, vision, and hope.

“At Pamoja Outreach Foundation, we meet families at very difficult points in their lives. Many carry burdens that are hidden from public view. Our responsibility is to listen carefully, respond thoughtfully, and remain present even after the first visit is over.

We do not promise what we cannot sustain. Instead, we focus on steady, realistic support that protects dignity and restores hope over time. Each child, each elderly person, and each household we encounter is more than a number—they are our neighbors.

My commitment as Director is to lead this work with integrity, accountability, and compassion so that anyone who chooses to walk with us in the future can be confident that their support is handled with care.”

Mr. Akuma Jamal
Founder & Director, Pamoja Outreach Foundation
RECENT WORKS

Recent Works

A glimpse into our recent community initiatives and impactful projects.

Orphanage visit

Learn more

Consistent visits provide encouragement, safe recreation, and essential supplies to children in care. Simple games, reading circles, and shared songs help restore a sense of normalcy and connection.

We coordinate closely with caregivers to understand needs, avoid duplication, and ensure dignity in delivery. When appropriate, we make referrals for medical, nutritional, or counseling support and return for follow-up.

The goal is predictable presence and practical help—small, steady acts that build trust and brighten daily routines.

Charity in Kiwempe, Kampala

Learn more

Targeted outreach in Kiwempe supports families with food packs, hygiene kits, and school materials, prioritizing households with children and elderly members.

Local leaders and trusted community contacts help us verify urgent needs and plan responsible distribution. We gather feedback after each visit to improve future support and reduce gaps.

This approach keeps assistance calm, fair, and respectful—focused on dignity and practical impact.

Ghetto child development sessions

Learn more

Structured sessions build confidence, literacy, and life skills for children in informal settlements. Activities include reading practice, creative arts, and simple problem-solving games.

Safe spaces and mentorship reduce risks and strengthen positive routines after school. Parents and guardians are kept involved through short check-ins and shared resources.

Measured attendance and small milestones ensure progress is steady and meaningful.

Charity outside Kampala

Learn more

Rural outreach extends support beyond the city, focusing on hard-to-reach households where basic items and services are scarce.

Collaboration with local clinics and churches improves access to care and follow-up. We aim to reduce travel burdens by organizing support days that combine multiple services.

Each visit is planned with accountability, clear records, and gentle communication.

Community bringing together

Learn more

Neighborhood events bring families together for information sharing, referrals, and mutual support. Short talks cover health, education continuity, and safety in the home.

Practical assistance and trust-building help communities recover and grow. We collect contact details for follow-up and connect households to specific services.

The emphasis is calm organization, clear guidance, and consistent presence.
